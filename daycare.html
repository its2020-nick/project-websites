Daycare & Kids with Autism

A safe, loving, and inclusive space for every child to thrive

Home Daycare & Autism
At Wekan Kasarani School, we believe every child deserves a nurturing start. Our Daycare programme and dedicated Autism Support unit provide specialised care and early learning in a warm, inclusive environment.

Our Programmes

Tailored support for every child's unique journey

Daycare Programme

Our full-day and half-day daycare provides a safe, stimulating environment for children from 6 months to 4 years. Qualified caregivers ensure each child receives the attention and early stimulation they need to grow and flourish.

Autism Support Unit

Our inclusive Autism Support Unit is staffed by trained special needs educators who use evidence-based approaches — including structured teaching, sensory integration, and play therapy — to support learners on the autism spectrum.

Parent & Family Support

We partner closely with families through regular consultations, progress reports, and workshops to ensure continuity of care between school and home.

Daycare Features

  • Full-day (7:00 AM – 5:00 PM) and half-day options
  • Qualified early childhood caregivers
  • Nutritious snacks and meals provided
  • Age-appropriate play and learning activities
  • Safe, child-proofed indoor and outdoor spaces
  • Daily routine with structured rest periods
  • Regular developmental progress updates to parents
  • Low child-to-caregiver ratio for personalised attention
  • Smooth transition support into PP1
  • Sensory play corners and reading nooks
  • Music, art, and movement sessions
  • CCTV-monitored classrooms for child safety

Autism Support Approach

Inclusive education that celebrates every learner

Structured Teaching

Clear, consistent routines and visual supports help learners understand expectations and build independence.

Sensory Integration

Tailored sensory activities help children regulate their responses to the environment and improve focus.

Communication Support

We use speech therapy techniques and augmentative communication tools to support verbal and non-verbal learners.

Social Skills Development

Group activities and peer inclusion programmes help learners build friendships and social confidence.
